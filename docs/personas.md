User Personas – DoctorShift Planner
Persona 1: ER On-Call Resident Physician

Name (fictional): Sarah
Age range: Late 20s
Work environment: Emergency Department (Hospital-based)
Work pattern: Rotating shifts (Day / Evening / Night), irregular weekly schedule

Daily Reality

Finishes night shifts exhausted and needs recovery time, yet often struggles to plan sleep and personal tasks properly.

Rebuilds her weekly plan manually every time new shifts are released.

Uses a generic digital calendar that does not account for post-shift recovery or fatigue.

Core Problems

No clear structure for when she should rest after night shifts.

Personal commitments often clash with recovery periods.

Mental fatigue caused by repetitive re-planning.

What She Needs From the System

Enter shift schedule once and rely on it as the planning baseline.

Enforce recovery rules after night shifts.

Automatically generate a realistic weekly plan instead of starting from scratch.

MVP Feature Mapping

Shift management

Recovery rules

Weekly plan generation

Conflict detection

Persona 2: Urgent Care Physician With Partial On-Call Schedule

Name (fictional): Khalid
Age range: Mid 30s
Work environment: Private Urgent Care Center
Work pattern: Evening shifts combined with fixed off-duty days

Daily Reality

Balances clinical work with family responsibilities.

Needs a clear overview of the entire week to avoid overloading specific days.

Planning mistakes usually appear the day after a shift.

Core Problems

Underestimates the impact of a shift on the following day.

Schedules demanding tasks too close to work hours.

Ends up with uneven workload distribution across the week.

What He Needs From the System

A clear weekly calendar view.

Automatic prevention of demanding tasks immediately after shifts.

Rule-based conflict warnings instead of generic reminders.

MVP Feature Mapping

Commitment management

Weekly planning

Conflict detection

Calendar visualization

Design Notes

Neither persona expects medical advice, health tracking, or diagnostics.

The goal is to reduce planning overhead, not to push productivity.

All decisions are based on user-defined rules, not assumptions.
